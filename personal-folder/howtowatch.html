How to Watch

General Conference features inspired speakers who share messages of faith, guidance, and encouragement with the worldwide congregation. Over the years, these talks have become cherished sources of inspiration, offering timeless wisdom applicable to various aspects of life. Here are glimpses of talks from some prominent speakers in previous conferences: President Russell M. Nelson: Talk Title: "The Book of Mormon: What Would Your Life Be Like without It?" Key Message: President Nelson emphasized the transformative power of the Book of Mormon in individual lives, inviting members to deepen their study and understanding of this sacred text. Elder Jeffrey R. Holland: Talk Title: "Be Ye Therefore Perfect—Eventually" Key Message: Elder Holland's talk focused on the journey of progression and growth, reassuring listeners that while perfection may be a distant goal, the pursuit of becoming better each day is both attainable and divinely encouraged. Sister Jean B. Bingham: Talk Title: "United in Accomplishing God’s Work" Key Message: Sister Bingham emphasized the power of unity and collaboration in accomplishing God's work, encouraging members to support and uplift one another in their shared discipleship. Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf: Talk Title: "Your Great Adventure" Key Message: Elder Uchtdorf spoke about the adventure of mortality, inviting listeners to embrace the challenges and uncertainties of life with faith, hope, and gratitude. President Dallin H. Oaks: Talk Title: "Love Your Enemies" Key Message: President Oaks explored the profound principle of loving one's enemies, drawing from the teachings of Jesus Christ to inspire a spirit of compassion and understanding. Elder Bednar: Talk Title: "Meek and Lowly of Heart" Key Message: Elder Bednar emphasized the importance of cultivating meekness and humility, showcasing these qualities as essential attributes of a disciple of Christ. Sister Sharon Eubank: Talk Title: "By Union of Feeling We Obtain Power with God" Key Message: Sister Eubank focused on the power of unity, emphasizing that when individuals and communities are united in purpose and feeling, they can access greater spiritual strength.

Attending or watching General Conference of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints provides a unique opportunity to receive guidance, inspiration, and teachings from church leaders. Whether you are a member of the LDS Church or someone seeking to learn more, here's a guide on how to watch or attend General Conference: In-Person Attendance: Location: General Conference is typically held at the Conference Center in Salt Lake City, Utah. If you plan to attend in person, check the official Church website for information on dates and ticketing. Tickets: While tickets are free, they are required for admission due to limited seating. Members can obtain tickets through their local leaders or online, depending on the region. Arrival: Plan to arrive early as sessions can be well-attended. Be mindful of security measures and any specific guidelines provided by event organizers. 2. Virtual Attendance: Official Broadcast: General Conference is broadcasted live on the official Church website. Visit the website to access the live stream during the scheduled sessions. Social Media: The Church often provides updates and links to live streams on its official social media channels, including Facebook, YouTube, and Twitter. Mobile Apps: The Church offers mobile apps for iOS and Android devices that allow users to watch live broadcasts. Download the app from the respective app store and follow the instructions for accessing the live stream. 3. Local Meetinghouses or Chapels: Broadcast at Local Chapels: Many local meetinghouses and chapels broadcast General Conference sessions. Check with your local church leaders for information on viewing opportunities in your area. Stake or Ward Gatherings: Some congregations organize gatherings at local chapels to watch General Conference together. This fosters a sense of community and shared spiritual experience. 4. Television and Radio: Local Broadcasts: Depending on your location, General Conference may be available on local television or radio stations. Check with local listings for broadcast details. BYUtv: Brigham Young University's television network (BYUtv) also broadcasts General Conference. Check their schedule for details on when and how to tune in. 5. On-Demand Viewing: Archived Sessions: If you are unable to watch live, General Conference sessions are typically archived on the official Church website. You can access these recordings at your convenience. YouTube Channel: The Church maintains an official YouTube channel where General Conference sessions are often uploaded for on-demand viewing.
